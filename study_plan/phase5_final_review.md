## 📋 Test Day Checklist

### The Night Before
- [ ] Bag packed with all required items (see below)
- [ ] Outfit laid out (layers — test rooms can be cold)
- [ ] Alarm set for 6:30 AM (or earlier if needed for travel)
- [ ] Phone plugged in to charge
- [ ] Light, healthy dinner eaten
- [ ] In bed by 10 PM — no screens after 9 PM

### What to Bring
- [ ] **Admission ticket** (printed)
- [ ] **Photo ID** (school ID, driver's license, or passport)
- [ ] **#2 pencils** (at least 3, sharpened)
- [ ] **Approved calculator** (with fresh batteries)
- [ ] **Watch** (no smartwatch — simple analog or digital)
- [ ] **Snack** for break (granola bar, banana, trail mix)
- [ ] **Water bottle**
- [ ] **Backup pencils and eraser**

### Morning of Test Day
- [ ] Wake up at 6:30 AM
- [ ] Eat a balanced breakfast (protein + carbs — eggs, toast, banana)
- [ ] Drink water (not too much caffeine)
- [ ] Review cheat sheet ONE time (5 minutes max) — then put it away
- [ ] Leave home with plenty of time (arrive by 7:45 AM)
- [ ] Deep breaths in the car / on the way

### At the Test Center
- [ ] Arrive by 7:45 AM (doors often close at 8 AM)
- [ ] Find your room and seat
- [ ] Put away your phone (OFF, not silent)
- [ ] Arrange pencils, calculator, water on desk
- [ ] Take 3 deep breaths before the test begins

### During the Test — Reminders
- [ ] Read each question carefully — don't rush
- [ ] Use POE on every question
- [ ] If stuck for >90 seconds, mark it and move on
- [ ] Use your break wisely: bathroom, snack, stretch, breathe
- [ ] Don't think about the previous section — focus forward
- [ ] Trust your preparation — you've done this hundreds of times in practice
